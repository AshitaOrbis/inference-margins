Reception phase — synthesis & resolution ledger (2026-07-11)

What this is: a simulated-reader exercise — nine independent GPT-5.6 Sol (xhigh) audit runs against the frozen v2.1.2 preview (deploy 201b22a7, git 9f1fd0f), designed per the plan reviews: five corpus-bounded source-faithfulness audits (third person, no role-play, no approval outputs) and four anonymous audience archetypes. What this is not: validation. These are model-generated adversarial findings with no established error rate; the absence of a finding is not evidence of correctness, a null result is bounded by the manifest-selected corpus, and this exercise does not satisfy the still-open 5-human-reader comprehension test. No aggregate approval score exists or will be published. Raw artifacts (verbatim): prompts + corpus manifest + all nine raw outputs · run metadata in each file's header. Synthesis rule: findings were deduplicated and dispositioned; none were upgraded, and every row links back to a raw output.

Headline

The audits re-ran the deployed suites and byte-checked the deployed assets against the frozen commit (both green; the anti-lens-shopping span, the three-deployment separation in §2c, the xAI segment-margin handling, and the $5B→$15B labeling all drew explicit "no contradiction found in this corpus") — and then found 16 P0-class defects and ~26 P1s the two prior review rounds had missed. The strongest theme, hit independently by five audits: attribution boundaries existed in expanded dossiers but vanished at collapsed point-of-use surfaces (selector names, hero zone labels, preset notes, ledger source cells). The single most damaging class: the calculator's own voice converting page-authored choices into source-attributed facts.

Convergent findings (multiple independent audits)

  • Hero "zone" labels mechanically attributed page-computed states to named people (TeorTaxes, Zephyr, Dylan Patel, fleetingbits audits + accounting archetype — 5/9 runs). Fixed: bands are now descriptive of cited ranges, never named; analyst presets carry an always-visible SITE-AUTHORED RECONSTRUCTION prefix on both the note and the hero.
  • Reconstruction labeling strong when expanded, absent when collapsed (4 named audits). Fixed: selector renames to [analyst reconstruction] …, boundary line in the collapsed preset note, per-parameter "Page-set X; no value in the cited corpus" source cells.
  • Point-of-use metric honesty (accounting + calibration + lurker). Fixed: metric renamed to modeled unit direct-serving contribution margin with an exact cost-perimeter statement; hero label says "not company GM"; whole-point ≈ display with the unrounded value as an inspectable diagnostic.

Final-gate re-audit — round 2 (2026-07-11, later)

After the first remediation round, the release went to its final gate (a four-persona council + GPT-5.6 Pro, blind-first). The council returned NO-SHIP: four of the dispositions below were overstated as first closed — ACC-1 (metric rename incomplete in the tooltip/§5/§6), MLI-1 (the sanitizer blocked the exact audit payload but not a schema-downgrade forgery or out-of-range/enum/blend values), MLI-8 (a warning is not an exit; ticks/radios/selects/blend/URL mutations bypassed it), LUR-5 (a printed commit is not reproducibility — the stamp step itself dirtied the tree) — and the MLI-5 deferral was challenged (an 86.2/76.8/61.5% Opus envelope is material). A second remediation round closed all five: token-prefix schema authority with disagreement rejection; full range/enum/blend-shape validation; divergent overlays and every edit path (sliders, ticks, radios, selects, blend, URL overlays, saved-preset loads) now EXIT replay identity to an explicit "[modified scenario]" state; v2 links and browser presets recover named-profile identity on exact match; the metric rename completed; the cost-lens span computes at the resolved replay traffic; a "Billable cached-input share" slider makes the cache-conflation assumption breakable (default remains the labeled assumed-equal); and releases are now reproducible (clean-tree gate, release-stamp commit, served-asset hash manifest at /asset-manifest.json). An application-level browser suite (tests/run-app-tests.sh) encodes the live attack payloads as permanent regressions. The rows below carry their round-2 status.
